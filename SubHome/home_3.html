Exercise 3: Given $A \rightarrow (C \rightarrow B)$, $C$.
Derive $A \rightarrow B$



  • Totally no idea about how to start?
    •
    First, you can set up an initial framework, which includes the given information and the final derivation.
    $ \begin{array}{ll} 1.\large|&A \rightarrow (C \rightarrow B) & ~\textup{given}\\ \hline 2.\large|&C& ~\textup{given}\\ \hline &\textup{...} \\ \hline .. \large|& A \rightarrow B& \textup{...}\\ \hline \end{array} $

    So, in the first few lines you see the given formulas on separate lines.
    The final line has the solution.
    Note that we don't know how many lines the rest will occupy!

  • Known that OR rule should be applied, but lost your goal when using OR rule?
    • From the final derivation, it is evident that B follows if A is given.
    However, A is not given. Hence, to arrive at the final conclusion, the assumption of A is necessary. Besides, at the last line, $I_{\rightarrow}$ should be applied.
    \[ \begin{array}{ll} 1.\large|&A \rightarrow (C \rightarrow B) & ~\textup{given}\\ \hline 2.\large|&C& ~\textup{given}\\ \hline 3.~~\large|&A & ~\textup{assume}\\ \hline &\textup{...}\\ \hline .. \large|& A \rightarrow B& \textup{$I_{\rightarrow}(3,...)$}\\ \hline \end{array} \]

  • Unable to go on after correctly finishing line 3(which is in Hint 2)?
    • To move on, it's essential to have a clear objective. Given the task of deriving B with A as a given condition, the second-to-last line should be B.
    Besides, according to the first three lines, only lines 1 and 3 can be applied by the rules that you have learned.
    \[ \begin{array}{ll} 1.\large|&A \rightarrow (C \rightarrow B) & ~\textup{given}\\ \hline 2.\large|&C& ~\textup{given}\\ \hline 3.~~~~\large|&A & ~\textup{assume}\\ \hline 4.~~~~\large|&C \rightarrow B& E_{\rightarrow}(1,3)\\ \hline 5.~~~~\large|&B& ~\textup{...}\\ \hline 6. \large|& A \rightarrow B& I_{\rightarrow}(3,5)\\ \hline \end{array} \]
